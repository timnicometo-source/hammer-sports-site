A clear path to getting started.

Hammer Sports Officials is always looking for new, dedicated individuals to join our team. While becoming an official with us is a straightforward process, it does require completing several important steps to ensure you are fully prepared and certified for the season.

Please follow the guide below to begin your journey. Whether you are brand new to officiating or returning with previous experience, Hammer Sports is here to help you get connected, prepared, and ready to work.

How It Works

Three steps to begin officiating.

Submit your application, complete onboarding, and prepare for assignments through Hammer Sports.

01

Submit Your Application

The first step is to express your interest by filling out our new official application . Please provide as much detail as possible, including your sporting experience and availability.

Once your application is received, a member of the Hammer Sports Officials leadership team will review it and respond by email within a few business days with next steps.

02

Complete Onboarding Paperwork

After your application has been approved, you will need to complete your initial onboarding. This includes a few required setup items so we can add you to our assigning and payment process.

  • Complete a W-9 Form: Download a W-9 Form , complete the required information, save the document as a PDF and upload your completed W-9 form here . As an independent contractor, you must complete and submit a W-9 form for payment purposes.
  • Set up your ArbiterSports account: You should receive an email inviting you to join our group. Our Arbiter name is Hammer Sports LLC and the group number is 104537.
  • Get help if needed: If you need help setting up your ArbiterSports account, contact Hammer Sports and we will help point you in the right direction.
03

Obtain Optional Certifications

All officials are strongly encouraged to hold valid certifications to be eligible for game assignments. If you plan to officiate high school level games, you must register with the Minnesota State High School League. This is a separate process with its own requirements and deadlines.

MYAS is another option to get certified in youth sports. Officials who obtain certification may have priority over those who do not because certification helps confirm eligibility, background checks, concussion training, and knowledge of current rules.

To learn more about ArbiterSports eligibility and registration, visit:
https://app.arbitersports.com/eligibility/official
